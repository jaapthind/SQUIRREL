Hi, I'm Jaap.

This entire project started right here in Berkeley because my dad was incredibly frustrated.

Every year, we would wait for our backyard fruit trees to ripen. And every year, the local squirrel population would raid the branches, ruining the harvest right before it was perfectly ready to eat. Traditional metal cones from the hardware store were ugly, didn't fit right, and often damaged the bark.

I wanted to solve the problem for him. Using my CAD software and my Bambu Lab A1 Mini 3D printer in my room, I began prototyping a modular, wrap-around barrier. We went through dozens of iterations, testing different friction coefficients and slip angles.

Once we found the perfect geometric angle that squirrels simply couldn't grip, our fruit was finally safe. Now, I manufacture these kits locally to help other families in Berkeley protect their hard work.

Deployment Protocol

No heavy tools required. Secure your harvest in under 15 minutes.

Inventory Your Kit

Before beginning, lay out all parts. Your kit includes: 6 Standard Plates, 2 Hook Anchor Plates, 16 Stainless Bolts/Nuts, 32 Washers, 1 Galvanized Metal Base Sheet, and 1 Roll of Heavy-Duty Packing Tape.

1

Establish the Foundation

Determine the optimal height (minimum 4 feet from ground). Wrap the galvanized metal sheet loosely upward around the trunk. Seal the bottom-most edge tightly against the bark using 3 sequential layers of the provided tape.

2

Anchor Plate Seating

Locate the 2 primary anchor plates featuring internal hooks. Secure the hooks directly over the top edge of the taped metal sheet. Position them exactly 180 degrees apart (opposite sides of the trunk) to balance the load.

3

Interlock & Torque

Attach standard plates to the hooks using 2 bolts per hole. Crucial: Ensure the "U" shaped slot on each side plate faces the opposing side's "U" hole. Hardware stack: Bolt → Washer → Plates → Washer → Nut.

4

Complete the Radial Barrier

Bridge the remaining gaps with the final middle plates to complete the 360-degree ring. Apply final torque to all stainless steel nuts using a wrench or pliers to achieve a rigid, monolithic structure.
