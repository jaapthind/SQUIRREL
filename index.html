Why the Interlocking System Works

Modular Adaptability

Unlike fixed-size metal cones from hardware stores, our interlocking plate design scales dynamically. Whether your tree trunk is 3 inches or 10 inches in diameter, the system wraps and secures tightly.

From Prototype to Production

Hi, my name is Jaap.

I originally designed this product because my dad kept complaining about squirrels raiding our backyard fruit trees. They always seemed to ruin the harvest right before the fruit was perfectly ripe to eat.

I decided to solve the problem myself. I started designing custom geometries and prototyping the parts on my Bambu Lab A1 Mini 3D printer right in my room. We went through dozens of iterations, testing angles and slip-factors.

Step-by-Step Installation

Before beginning, layout all the parts on a flat surface to ensure you have everything.

  • 6 Standard Baffle Plates
  • 2 Primary Hook Plates
  • 16 Stainless Steel Bolts
  • 16 Stainless Steel Nuts
  • 32 Stainless Steel Washers
  • 1 Galvanized Metal Foundation Sheet
  • 1 Roll Heavy-Duty Tape
1
Wrap Metal Sheet around the Trunk

From the base of the tree, wrap the metal sheet loosely upwards around the trunk, overlapping the edges slightly. This creates a smooth foundation that protects the bark.

At the bottom of the trunk, make sure the sheet is oriented pointing upwards, and seal that bottom edge tightly with at least 3 overlapping layers of the provided tape.

2
Mount the Plates with Hooks

Locate the 2 primary plates that feature the internal hook mechanism. Insert the hook of each plate firmly over the top edge of the metal sheet you just taped to the tree.

  • Position the two hook plates on exactly opposing sides of the trunk (180 degrees apart).
  • Ensure they are hanging evenly before moving to the next step.
3
Attach Remaining Plates

Attach 2 standard plates to each of the hook plates you just hung. Use 2 bolts for each connection hole. Stack: Bolt -> Washer -> Plates -> Washer -> Nut.

CRITICAL: Make sure the “U” shaped slot on each side plate is facing the opposing side’s “U” hole. This geometry creates the slide-off effect.

Finally, attach the remaining plates into the middle gaps to complete the 360-degree barrier. Ensure all hardware is fully tightened with a wrench so the cone is rigid.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will the installation harm my tree's growth? +

Not at all. The underlying galvanized metal sheet distributes the pressure of the mounting hooks, preventing any metal from digging into the sensitive bark or cambium layer. As your tree grows in girth over the years, you can simply loosen the tape and expand the system.

What if squirrels jump from a nearby fence or roof? +

This barrier is highly effective at stopping ground-up climbing. However, squirrels are incredible jumpers (capable of leaping 8-10 feet horizontally). For full protection, ensure your tree canopy is pruned at least 8 feet away from fences, roofs, or unbaffled neighboring trees.

Does it work against raccoons or rats? +

Yes! While geometrically optimized for the climbing mechanics of Eastern Gray and Fox Squirrels, the slick surface and severe downward angle are equally effective at preventing rats, raccoons, and even neighborhood cats from ascending the trunk.

Can I paint the baffle to match my tree or landscaping? +

Absolutely. The industrial polymer takes spray paint quite well. We recommend lightly scuffing the surface with fine sandpaper, then using an outdoor-rated, plastic-bonding spray paint (like Rust-Oleum or Krylon). Choose a matte brown, bark-texture, or olive green to camouflage the baffle.
