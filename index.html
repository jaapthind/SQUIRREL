The Story Behind the Design

Hi, my name is Jaap.

I created this product because my dad kept complaining about squirrels raiding our fruit trees and ruining the harvest right before the fruit was perfectly ripe to eat.

To solve his problem, I utilized my background in engineering and design to model a custom, wrap-around squirrel-proof barrier. After prototyping and testing multiple iterations using precision 3D printing techniques right here in Berkeley, I found the perfect geometry.

It worked flawlessly for my family, instantly saving our avocados and stone fruit. Now, I manufacture these kits locally to share with your family.

Why Choose the Stop Squirrel Climb System?

Modular Adaptability

Unlike fixed-size metal cones, our patent-pending interlocking plate design scales dynamically. Whether your tree is 3 inches or 12 inches in diameter, the system wraps and secures tightly.

Humane Deterrence

No traps, no poison, no spikes. The steep, smooth angle simply prevents the squirrel from gripping, causing them to slide safely back to the ground.

Weather-Resistant Materials

Constructed using industrial-grade UV-resistant polymers and rust-proof stainless steel hardware. Engineered to withstand intense summer sun, heavy rain, and freezing winters without degrading.

Tree-Safe Grip

The included flexible metal foundation sheet prevents the mounting hardware from biting into the bark, protecting the cambium layer and ensuring your tree remains healthy.

Step-by-Step Installation Instructions

This system stops squirrels from climbing tree trunks, protecting your fruit and bird feeders.

Parts included in your kit:

  • 6 standard baffle plates
  • 2 primary plates with mounting hooks
  • 16 stainless steel bolts
  • 16 stainless steel nuts
  • 32 stainless steel washers
  • 1 wrap-around galvanized metal foundation sheet
  • 1 roll of heavy-duty packing tape
Note: The metal sheet is not shown in this image but is included in your set.
1. Wrap Metal Sheet around the Tree Trunk
This is what the bottom of the metal sheet should look like.

From the base of the tree, wrap the metal sheet loosely upwards around the trunk, overlapping the edges slightly. This creates a smooth foundation that protects the bark.

At the bottom of the trunk, make sure the sheet is oriented pointing upwards, and seal that bottom edge tightly with at least 3 overlapping layers of the provided tape.

  • Ensure the sheet is snug but allows a tiny bit of flex for tree growth.
  • Secure it entirely in place using tape before proceeding.
2. Mount the Plates with Hooks

Locate the 2 primary plates that feature the internal hook mechanism. Insert the hook of each plate firmly over the top edge of the metal sheet you just taped to the tree.

  • Position the two hook plates on exactly opposing sides of the trunk (180 degrees apart).
  • Ensure the plates feel secure and are hanging evenly before moving to the next step.
3. Attach Remaining Non-Hook Plates
Both sides assembled
Side view detail

Now, attach 2 standard plates to each of the hook plates you just hung. Use 2 bolts for each connection hole. Remember the hardware stack: Bolt -> Washer -> Plates -> Washer -> Nut.

  • CRITICAL: Make sure the “U” shaped slot on each side plate is facing the opposing side’s “U” hole. This geometry is what creates the slide-off effect.
Filling the middle gap to complete the ring

Finally, attach the remaining plates into the middle gaps between your sub-assemblies to complete the 360-degree barrier.

  • Use 2 bolts, 4 washers, and 2 nuts for each interlocking hole.
  • Ensure all bolts and nuts are fully tightened with pliers or a wrench so the cone is rigid.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this harm my tree? +

Not at all. The underlying galvanized metal sheet distributes the pressure of the mounting hooks, preventing any metal from digging into the sensitive bark or cambium layer. As your tree grows over the years, you can simply loosen the tape and expand the system.

What if squirrels jump from a nearby fence or roof? +

This barrier is highly effective at stopping ground-up climbing. However, squirrels are incredible jumpers (capable of leaping 8-10 feet horizontally). For full protection, ensure your tree branches are pruned at least 8 feet away from fences, roofs, or unbaffled neighboring trees.

Does it work against raccoons or rats? +

Yes! While optimized for the climbing mechanics of Eastern Gray and Fox Squirrels, the slick surface and downward angle are equally effective at preventing rats, raccoons, and neighborhood cats from ascending the trunk.

Can I paint it to match my tree? +

Yes. The PETG plastic takes spray paint quite well. We recommend using an outdoor-rated, plastic-bonding spray paint (like Rust-Oleum or Krylon). Choose a matte brown or green to camouflage the baffle into your landscaping.

Where is this made? +

Every single kit is designed, printed, assembled, and shipped directly from my workshop in Berkeley, California. By buying this kit, you are supporting independent engineering and local fabrication.
